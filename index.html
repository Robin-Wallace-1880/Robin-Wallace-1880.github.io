Custodian account structure

Your assets are held in a custodian account in your name. That means clear ownership, independent reporting, and transparency.

  • Assets are not “on our balance sheet”.
  • You can see what you own and where it is held.
  • Clear separation between advice/management and custody.

FAQ

Short answers — happy to go deeper on a call
What do you invest in?

Typically low-cost, diversified instruments (e.g., broad market funds/ETFs). The exact implementation depends on your location, platform, and suitability.

How often do you rebalance?

Rebalancing is done when it makes sense to maintain the intended risk level — not in response to headlines.

Do you try to beat the market?

No. The philosophy is to capture market returns over time, keep costs low, and avoid behavioural mistakes.

What does “custodian account” mean?

Your assets are held in your name at an independent custodian. This provides transparency and clear ownership.

Can I add or withdraw money?

Generally yes, subject to product/platform rules and any applicable notice periods. We’ll explain the mechanics before you start.

Is this financial advice?

No. This website provides general information only. Any advice would require a proper suitability/appropriateness process.
